Plastic pollution in the ocean.

According to the World Wildlife Forum, there's been an estimate of around 100.000 marine mammals being killed every year, due to ingestion or entanglement in plastic artifacts. Though not all species are affected, the WWF states that all seven sea turtle species are affected by the pollution. To sealife, it can be hard to distinguish a plastic bag from a jellyfish or other prey which in result can cost it it's life (2021).

The United Nations aim to help the ocean by reducing ocean waste from humans and industrial process. Goal number 14 aims to free the ocean from plastic pollution and human waste ,they use ten "criteria" in order to fulfill the goal. The goal is to reduce marine pollution and make sustaibale fishing a reality while protect and conserve ecosystems and coasttal areas, just to mention a few. More then 8 million tonnes of plastic enters the ocean every year, and The United Nations tends to put an end to it.

What can YOU do to help the ocean? Ocean plastic pollution will triple by 2040, if we dont change our habbits.

  • Dont go throw plastic in the ocean when your to lazy to dispose of it in a appropriate way. Leave nothing behind!
  • Cut down on what you throw away!
  • Dont buy products that can harm the ocean climate. Example are some sunscreen that have some chemicals that changes the DNA in the coral reefs.
  • Recycle plastic products like a plastic bottle or use a plastic bag over several shopping trips.
